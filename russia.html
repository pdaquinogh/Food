Pelmeni

Pelmeni are traditional Russian dumplings consisting of a thin dough filled with a mixture of minced meat, which can include pork, beef, lamb, or chicken, often combined with onions and spices. They are a staple of Russian cuisine, enjoyed with a variety of condiments such as sour cream, vinegar, or mustard.

Simple Pelmeni Recipe

Ingredients

    For the Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • For the Filling:
  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
    • For Serving:
  • Sour cream
  • Fresh herbs (such as dill or parsley), chopped

Instructions

Make the Dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Make a well in the center, add the egg and water, and mix to combine. Knead until the dough is smooth and elastic. Cover with a cloth and let rest for 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling: In another bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, chopped onion, garlic (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly.

Form the Pelmeni: Roll out the dough on a floured surface until thin. Using a glass or cookie cutter, cut out circles. Place a small amount of filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges to seal, then bring the two corners together and pinch to make a rounded dumpling shape.

Cook the Pelmeni: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pelmeni in batches, stirring gently to prevent sticking. Cook until they float to the surface and the dough is tender, about 3 to 5 minutes.

Serve: Serve hot with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.
